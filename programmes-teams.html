Creating Healthy Teams

The Aim

The principal aim of driving performance at a team level should be based on a shared ownership and accountability model. It creates a more collaborative, aligned and connected approach to team tasks that increases motivation, engagement, and performance.
This collective responsibility also reduces conflict and discourages employees from operating in silos, which can be deeply damaging to your business.
Put simply, it is for the team (with complementary skills sets) to take control of their own destiny. A level of autonomy that creates strong and successful teams.
Much of the work undertaken with teams should be based on relationship intelligence. The focus here, is to form better connections using ongoing insight to gauge the common themes, trends and needs that lie at the very heart of your teams.
